About Me

My path into data engineering runs through two disciplines that rarely meet in one person: the engineering rigor to build reliable data infrastructure, and the decision-science training,through an MSc in Operations Research at the University of Lagos - to understand what that infrastructure ultimately serves.

Operations Research trains me to model complexity: how do I allocate limited resources across competing demands? How do I plan under uncertainty? How do I find an optimal path through a system with dozens of constraints? These are the same questions businesses wrestle with every day, and they're questions most data engineers never think about, because most data engineering roles stop at "deliver clean data." My interest goes further than that.

On the engineering side, I work across the full data stack ingestion, transformation, warehouse modeling, and the semantic layer where business logic gets codified into reusable assets. I care about data quality, lineage, and documentation that makes a codebase self-explanatory long after the original author has moved on.

What draws me to analytics engineering specifically is the translation layer - taking messy upstream data and shaping it into clean, well-tested models that stakeholders can actually trust. A dashboard built on bad models is worse than no dashboard at all. Getting that foundation right is where I focus.

My core toolkit spans Python, SQL, dbt, and data modeling - with growing depth in warehouse-native analytics, ELT architecture, and optimization-informed analytical design.

Last-Mile Logistics Optimization

Problem: Small businesses in emerging markets lacked structured data pipelines to support routing decisions and demand forecasting.

Approach: Built clustering-based demand zone models; engineered features for a rider assignment algorithm from raw transactional data - drawing on OR resource allocation logic to structure the optimization layer.

Results: Delivered a reproducible framework that converts unstructured delivery logs into optimized resource allocation inputs.

US Flights Data Pipeline & Analysis

Problem: Large-scale flight datasets were too inconsistent and unstructured to support reliable delay analysis or scheduling insights.

Approach: Designed a cleaning and transformation pipeline to standardize raw flight records; built visualizations surfacing delay distribution patterns across carriers and routes.

Results: Produced a trusted analytical dataset and dashboard layer that made peak delay periods and seasonal trends immediately accessible to non-technical stakeholders.

Python SQL Seaborn

Airline Customer Segmentation Model

Problem: Raw customer data existed in silos, making it impossible to identify behavioral patterns or model distinct traveler segments for downstream strategy.

Approach: Engineered customer features from transaction-level data; applied k-means clustering to surface statistically distinct segments - structuring outputs so marketing and product teams could query them directly.

Results: Reduced time-to-insight for loyalty program decisions; gave stakeholders a queryable segmentation layer that replaced ad-hoc analysis.
